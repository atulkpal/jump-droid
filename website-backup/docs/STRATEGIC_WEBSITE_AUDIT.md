## PHASE 1: WHAT IS JUMP DROID? (The Actual Game)

### To a Player
> Jump Droid is a **physics-based vertical platformer where you pilot customizable rockets upward through 6 atmospheric zones**. You manage three competing resources—fuel (thrust duration), heat (engine lockout timer), and shield (damage buffer)—while landing on platforms, building combo chains, and collecting survival rewards. Every 1500–18000 points, you face a boss with destructible weak points and multi-phase patterns. You unlock new rocket classes at score milestones (2K, 5K, 10K), customize them with 17 modular upgrades, and discover hidden lore entries that permanently expand your codex. The climb never ends, but the higher you go, the stranger reality becomes.

### To a Game Journalist
> Jump Droid is a **roguelike platformer hybrid with depth-first design philosophy**. Unlike score-chasing games, it uses a discovery-driven progression loop: completing specific challenges and reaching zones unlock codex entries that reward long-term exploration. The resource triangle (fuel ↔ heat ↔ shield ↔ hull) creates meaningful decisions every thrust. Boss encounters feature destructible weak points and multi-phase mechanics unusual for mobile platformers. The combo economy—where consecutive successful landings restore survival supplies—elegantly ties mastery to mechanical reward. Four rocket classes with 17 modules each create deep build variety for repeated playthroughs.

### To a Publisher
> **Market Position:** Tactical vertical exploration for core/hardcore mobile gamers seeking depth and narrative over reflexes.  
> **Differentiation:** Resource management + Boss complexity + Discovery system + Modular builds + Physics-based movement  
> **Progression Loop:** Score milestones → Rocket unlocks → Customization depth → Codex collection → Ascension ranking  
> **Content Depth:** 6 zones, 6 bosses (31 total threats), 4 rocket classes, 17 modules, 43 lore entries, 15 mission templates  
> **Retention Drivers:** Discovery rewards, boss mastery, build optimization, combo skill expression, lore collection  
> **Core Tension:** Every decision trades resource—fuel vs. shield vs. heat vs. momentum  

### To an Investor
> **What You're Funding:**  
> A vertically-scrolling platformer that combines roguelike structure, physics simulation, and discovery-driven progression. The game has shipped with all core systems implemented (6 zones, 6 bosses, 4 rockets, 43 discoveries, 31 threats, 17 modules). Revenue model (TBD: ad-supported, premium, cosmetics, BP) has proven mobile platformer precedent. Unique mechanics (resource triangle, combo economy, destructible bosses, modular builds) create defensible IP. The team has completed EPIC 7 and is roadmapped for EPIC 10 with 3 new zones, 4 new bosses, and new rocket classes.

#### What It Communicates Well ✅
- **Tagline:** "The Signal From the Void" is mysterious and sci-fi accurate

- **What do I actually DO?** Hero says "pilot" but doesn't explain "touch to thrust, manage fuel/heat, land on platforms"
- **Why is it different?** Hero mentions tactics and bosses but omits resource management and combo economy
- **Progression incentive:** No mention of rocket unlocks, score milestones, or discovery rewards
- **Visual proof:** No gameplay video, screenshot, or animated GIF showing actual play
- **Time commitment:** No signal of scope (6 zones? 1-hour campaign? Endless?)

#### Atmosphere vs. Conversion Trade-off
- **Verdict:** ATMOSPHERE WINS | Conversion loses slightly

- **Impact on conversion:** Doesn't signal long-term progression goal

#### 3. **Module Customization Depth** (Massive miss)
- **What it is:** 17 modules per rocket (68 total configurations): hull (+), shield (+), engine, heat management, utilities
- **Why it matters:** Deep build theory. "Min-max" appeal for core gamers.
- **Current website:** Shows static rockets. No hint of customization.
- **Impact on conversion:** Undersells replayability and build variety to target audience

- **What it is:** 6 unique bosses (Command Cruiser, Gatekeeper, Leviathan, Star-Eater, Void Engine, The Signal) each with:
  - 2–4 destructible weak points
  - Phase-based behavior shifts
  - Unique attack patterns (gravity pulses, energy shields, screen shrinks, etc.)
