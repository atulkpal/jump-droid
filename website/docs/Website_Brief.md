## 1. Documentation Findings & Game Understanding

Jump Droid is not a generic mobile platformer or a simple score chaser. Based on the repository analysis, it is a **Vertical Exploration Simulator** built with Jetpack Compose. 

### Core Pillars:
*   **The Ascent Narrative:** A journey from Earth's surface to the reality-warping "Void".
*   **Mechanical Depth:** A "Resource Management Triangle" (Fuel, Heat, Shield, Hull) that demands tactical precision, not just reflexes.
*   **Boss Confrontations:** Large-scale, multi-phase encounters with destructible weak points—rare for the genre.
*   **Discovery Economy:** A 43-entry Codex (Discovery System) that drives long-term engagement through lore and collection.
*   **Modular Customization:** Rocket "Classes" (Balanced, Scout, Tank, Experimental) that fundamentally change the gameplay loop.

## 4. Website Vision: "The Digital Ascent"

The website should not just *describe* the game; it should **mirror the game experience**. 
As the user scrolls down, they are visually **ascending** through the zones of the game.

### User Journey Phases:
1.  **Ignition (Hero):** Capture the curiosity of the "Great Signal".
2.  **The Ascent (Body):** Experience the atmospheric shift of each zone.
3.  **The Tech (Builds):** Showcase the modular rockets and tactical HUD.
4.  **The Signal (Call to Action):** Direct to download/join the expedition.

## 7. Zone-Based Storytelling Strategy

Each zone on the website should highlight a specific "Pain & Gain" of the gameplay:
*   **Earth:** Focus on ease of entry (Tutorial).
*   **Cloud Layer:** Highlight the "Wind & Turbulence" physics challenge.
*   **Orbit:** Showcase "The Gatekeeper" and the scale of Boss battles.
*   **Deep Space:** Emphasize the "Void Whale" and "Stalker" AI complexity.
*   **The Void:** Lean into the "Reality Distortion" and cryptic Lore.

---

## 8. Visual Design Direction

*   **Color Palette:** Use the game's internal `SciFi` color constants (Cyan, Gold, Purple, Red).
*   **Typography:** High-tech, mono-spaced headers (e.g., JetBrains Mono) paired with clean sans-serif body text.
*   **UI Elements:** Buttons and cards should mimic the game's HUD—minimalist borders, scan-line overlays, and slight "flicker" on hover.
*   **Motion:** Parallax background layers (Clouds, Stars, Debris) that move at different speeds relative to scroll.

## 13. Risks, Gaps and Recommendations

*   **Gap:** The "Combo System" is a key mastery mechanic but hard to explain in text. 
    *   *Recommendation:* Use a "Combo Counter" animation that ticks up as the user scrolls past platform-themed sections.
*   **Risk:** Performance on mobile browsers with heavy parallax. 
    *   *Recommendation:* Use CSS-only parallax and optimized SVGs for the zone backgrounds.
*   **Messaging Weakness:** "Jump Droid" name sounds generic.
    *   *Recommendation:* Aggressively brand the subtitle: "The Signal From the Void" or "Advanced Ascent Protocol".
